Coaching: George Karl

Many former ABA players have found success after playing through coaching at various levels of basketball. George Karl has had success at the highest levels. A former guard for the ABA’s San Antonio Spurs, Karl maintained a lengthy coaching career for many different teams. He began coaching as an assistant for the Spurs in 1978 and coached all the way up until recently in 2013. He coached predominantly in the NBA, but also for the European club Real Madrid as well as for the Albany Patroons in the Continental Basketball Association. During his career, Karl lead the Seattle Supersonics to the NBA Finals, coached the U.S. National Team in the FIBA World Championship, was named head coach in four different NBA All-Star games, and named NBA Coach of the Year in 2013. On December 10, 2010, he became the seventh coach in NBA history to record 1,000 wins. This combined with his 20 winning seasons, 21 playoff appearences, and 78 career playoff victories make him one of the all-time coaching legends of basketball.

Some other notable coaches who were former ABA members include Jim O’Brien, Larry Brown, Al Skinner, George Irvine, Dan Issel, & Hubie Brown.
