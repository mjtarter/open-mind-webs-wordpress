Success After Basketball: Charlie Scott

Charlie Scott was the first black scholarship athlete at the University of North Carolina in 1966. Although many parts of the south were still hostile towards African Americans, Scott found comfort at UNC and credits coach Dean Smith for helping him to succeed. Scott then went on to play in both the ABA and NBA, setting the All-Time ABA Record for Points Per Game at 34.6. His success was on more than just the basketball court though. After his playing days, Charlie served as marketing director for Champion (sports apparel), executive vice president of CTS (telemarketing firm), and operated his own business. Regarding his decision to leave the basketball world behind after his playing days and trying something new Scott states “I wanted to get out of basketball for a while. I think when you do something all your life, you want to make a change and take a little time off. I have the satisfaction of doing the things I wanted to. I may not have done them as well as I wanted to, but I did them and I enjoyed them and the record speaks for itself."
